The Format

The first-ever 48-team World Cup. Twelve groups of four. The top two from each group, plus the eight best third-place finishers, advance to a knockout Round of 32 — producing a longer, broader tournament across three host nations.

How Third Place Works

Eight of the 12 third-place finishers advance to the Round of 32 — ranked across all groups by:

  1. Points
  2. Goal difference
  3. Goals scored
  4. Fair play record
  5. FIFA ranking (if still tied)

For the first time ever, a team can finish third in its group and go on to win the trophy. The champion now needs 8 wins instead of 7.

New Rules for 2026
Anti-Time-Wasting
Referees visibly count down goal kicks and throw-ins. Exceed the limit and possession is awarded to the opponent.
Prompt Substitutions
Substituted or injured players must exit quickly — delays can hold the replacement from entering play.
Expanded VAR
VAR now reviews potential second yellows, mistaken identity, and plays leading up to goals or red cards — more chances to correct major errors.
Longer Stoppage Time
8–12 minutes of added time is now the norm, accounting for injuries, goal celebrations, substitutions, and VAR reviews.
